Privacy Policy

BARE by Marlese is committed to protecting and respecting your privacy in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Data Controller

BARE by Marlese
Email: marlese@barebymarlese.com

2. Information We Collect

We may collect and process the following information:

  • Personal details (name, email, phone number)
  • Fitzpatrick skin type / skin classification
  • Medical contraindications
  • Medication information
  • Consultation notes
  • Photographs taken before, during, or after treatment

3. Special Category Data

Some information collected (including medical details and skin type) is classified as Special Category Data under UK GDPR. This information is collected solely for the purpose of ensuring safe and appropriate treatment.

4. Purpose of Processing

Your data is processed for the following purposes:

  • Responding to enquiries
  • Providing consultation and treatment
  • Ensuring client safety
  • Maintaining treatment records

5. Legal Basis

We process your personal data based on:

  • Your explicit consent
  • Contractual necessity (providing booked treatments)
  • Legal obligations relating to health and safety

6. Data Storage & Security

Data is stored securely and accessed only when necessary. Form submissions are processed via Formspree (a secure third-party form processor). Reasonable steps are taken to protect your information from unauthorised access.

7. Data Retention

Consultation and treatment records may be retained for up to 7 years in accordance with insurance and legal requirements. Enquiry-only submissions may be deleted after 12 months if no treatment occurs.

8. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Request access to your data
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion (where legally permitted)
  • Withdraw consent at any time

To exercise your rights, contact: marlese@barebymarlese.com

9. Complaints

If you are unhappy with how your data is handled, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO).
